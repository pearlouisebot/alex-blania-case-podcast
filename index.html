About This Case Study

A comprehensive business case study examining Alex Blania's strategic challenge as the repeat co-founder of multiple AI-era companies. This complete 14-minute analysis explores the "third-founder problem" and the intersection of proof-of-human technology, social networks, and digital identity in an AI-abundant world.

The case examines Alex's unique position as operator of Sam Altman's most ambitious non-OpenAI bet: building the human layer of the internet through World's proof-of-human infrastructure, Merge Labs' brain-computer interfaces, and potentially Nara's social networking platform. This comprehensive version covers strategic options, token economics, interview insights, decision frameworks, and a proposed 180-day execution plan.

Topics Covered

  • The third-founder problem: managing World, Merge Labs, and Nara simultaneously
  • Alex Blania's background as physicist turned AI-era CEO
  • The Sam Altman connection and strategic partnership dynamics
  • Technical infrastructure vs. market adoption challenges
  • Proof-of-human technology and biometric identity verification
  • The future of social networks in an AI-abundant world
  • Strategic options for scaling the human layer of the internet
  • Execution risk vs. strategic opportunity analysis
